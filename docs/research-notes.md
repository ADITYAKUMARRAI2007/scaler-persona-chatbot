# Research Notes

These notes summarize public-source research used to shape the persona prompts. They are intentionally conservative because the assignment uses real people.

## Anshuman Singh

Public grounding:
- Co-founder of Scaler / InterviewBit.
- IIIT Hyderabad alumnus.
- Public sources describe him as a competitive programmer and two-time ACM ICPC World Finalist.
- Former Facebook/Meta engineer.
- Public positioning: creating 1M+ world-class engineers and bridging the gap between education and industry.

Prompt implications:
- Make the persona structured, practical, fundamentals-first, and interview-readiness oriented.
- Use themes like talent beyond college brand, peer learning, industry-vetted curriculum, and measurable outcomes.

- https://www.iiit.ac.in/scaler/

## Abhimanyu Saxena

Public grounding:
- Co-founder of InterviewBit and Scaler.
- IIIT Hyderabad alumnus.
- Public interviews mention Progress Software and Fab.com before InterviewBit/Scaler.
- Public themes include employment-focused tech education, practitioners teaching practitioners, mentorship, community, and measurable learning outcomes.

Prompt implications:
- Make the persona founder/operator-like: strategic, analytical, outcome-driven.
- Use cause-effect framing: university curriculum gap → industry skill gap → practitioner-led education.
- Push students toward proof of work, visible building, and right effort in the right direction.

- https://in.linkedin.com/in/abhimanyusaxena

## Kshitij Mishra

Public grounding:
- Public LinkedIn profile places him in Bengaluru, associated with Scaler and IIIT Hyderabad.
- Public posts describe InterviewBit Lead Engineer, teaching as Scaler Academy started, and contribution to Scaler School of Technology.
- Publicly described in Scaler School of Technology context as Dean.
- Google Scholar profile lists IIIT Hyderabad / Scaler Academy affiliation and technical publications.

Prompt implications:
- Make the persona warm, reflective, technical, and teacherly.
- Emphasize technical depth, long-term growth, learning through struggle, builder mindset, real users, product thinking, and venture-building.
- Avoid overclaiming exact current titles or private classroom content.
